Tiny Tails Rescue Adelaide

Incorporated is a not for profit rescue run by dedicated volunteers helping rescue unwanted dogs & cats and giving them a second chance.

Responsive Website Redesign

Our team’s goal was to redesign the NFP Tiny Tails website to enhance the site navigation, visual aesthetics and overall usability so that website visitors are more inclined to contact the organisation for more information, offer to volunteer or adopt a pet.

User Research

Interviews

"On Facebook you can see the pictures of the animals not text…."

As User researchers we wanted to understand the thought process and any pain points of pet adoption/fostering and supporting to local pet rescue charities.

Key Takeaways

  • Users love to adopt a pet.
  • They usually used sites like Google, FB or sites like RSPCA to browse pets.
  • They wanted to know more details about the pet.
  • They would prefer to donate online via card payment.

Surveys

Get a more broad understanding of the pet adoption thought process of users we conducted a survey We created a survey using Google forms , distributed via social media and received 44 responses These were the more significant insights gathered

User Persona

Meet Jess....
With the research gathered, a user persona was created to help guide our design decisions to ensure that the user’s needs were always met. Jess is a 28 year old barista and her goals and pain points helped lay the foundation of our redesign.

Problem statement


We believe that a delightful redesign of the Tiny Tails Rescue website that focuses on enhancing the navigation, visual aesthetics and responsive standards, would improve usability and attract visitors to donate, volunteer with the organisation or adopt a pet.

During the interview and the surveying process, we discovered that users are enthusiastic about adopting/fostering a pet and have previously or would in the future, donate to help a pet in need, however, they want to see where their funds go to justify their donation.

Therefore we believe that heart-warming stories about the animals in need must be prominent on the website so that users feel inclined to donate, volunteer or adopt/foster.

We might do this by providing pet profiles containing high resolution images, history and success stories which demonstrate how donation and adoptions have transformed their lives. Doing this will allow our product to attract adopters/foster carers volunteers and donors

Visual Design

When deciding on what colours to go with for the redesign, we decided we wanted to have a friendly feel and bubbly nature while still displaying a care or pets and chose colours to communicate just that.

There were some changes made to the final prototype based on the feedback from the usability testings

We came across a colour accessibility problem and detected it at a crucial time and adjusted the colours to match the AA standards for colour accessibility.

Responsive Design

Completing same tasks intuitively on a Smartphone, scaling down legibly and reordering information to suit the new screen size were some of our mobile responsive design goals.

Future Steps & Conclusion

We would like to continue testing to the prototype and understand any more changes that needs to be done. And in terms of the website it self some additions such as Success Stories Page, An updated blog, and subscriptions to newsletters will need to be implemented. Improving and developing the responsive design to suit the mobile screens in also one of the higher priority tasks
